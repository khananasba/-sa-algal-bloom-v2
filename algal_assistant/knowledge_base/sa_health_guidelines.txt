SA Health official Karenia thresholds for South Australia.
Danger threshold is 10000 cells per litre — beach closure recommended.
Caution threshold is 1000 cells per litre — exercise caution advised.
Safe is below 1000 cells per litre — swimming generally acceptable.
SA Health is the only official authority for beach safety decisions.
Karenia brevis causes neurotoxic shellfish poisoning in humans.
Symptoms include respiratory irritation, eye irritation, nausea, vomiting.
Do not eat shellfish fish or seafood from affected areas during bloom.
Do not swim surf or wade in water exceeding danger threshold.
Children and people with respiratory conditions are most at risk.
Pets should be kept away from affected beaches and water.
SA Health issues official advisories that Education Department acts on.
Schools must demonstrate duty of care under Education Act obligations.
Risk assessment documentation is required for all school outdoor activities.
