SA Health official Karenia thresholds for South Australia.
Danger threshold: 10000 cells per litre - beach closure recommended.
Caution threshold: 1000 cells per litre - exercise caution.
Safe: below 1000 cells per litre.
SA Health is the only official authority for beach safety decisions.
Karenia brevis causes neurotoxic shellfish poisoning in humans.
Symptoms include respiratory irritation, eye irritation, nausea, vomiting.
Do not eat shellfish, fish, or seafood from affected areas.
Do not swim, surf, or wade in affected waters during High or Critical alerts.
Children and people with respiratory conditions are most at risk.
Pets should be kept away from affected beaches.
