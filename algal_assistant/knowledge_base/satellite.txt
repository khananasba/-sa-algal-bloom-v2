Important note on data priority:
Ground truth water sampling is the most accurate data source.
SA Government field scientists collect water samples and count
Karenia cells under a microscope giving exact cell counts
in cells per litre at specific beach locations.
This platform has 509 ground truth readings from 512 SA beach
sites collected since September 2025.
Satellite SFABI readings are a supporting indicator that covers
the entire coastline but cannot measure exact cell counts.
When ground truth data is available for a specific beach always
use the cell count in cells per litre as the primary safety indicator.
Satellite data fills the gaps for areas between sampling sites
and gives advance warning before field sampling is conducted.

The SA Algal Bloom Monitor uses Sentinel-2 satellite imagery
processed via Google Earth Engine to detect algal blooms from space.
Sentinel-2 passes over South Australia every 5 days.
The platform automatically searches the last 6 months for the
most recent cloud-free satellite image.
The most recent satellite image processed was from May 8 2026
which is 2 days ago.
48 real Sentinel-2 satellite pixels were sampled across SA
coastal waters from this image.
Current SFABI readings from May 8 2026 satellite pass:
minimum SFABI 0.0202, maximum SFABI 0.4358, mean SFABI 0.1440.

SFABI Spectral Fluorescence Algal Bloom Index explained:
SFABI uses Sentinel-2 bands B2 blue 490nm, B3 green 560nm,
B6 red-edge 740nm, B7 red-edge 783nm, B12 shortwave infrared 2190nm.
Formula: SFABI equals B6 plus B7 minus B2 minus B3 minus B12
divided by B6 plus B7 plus B2 plus B3 plus B12.
Karenia brevis produces chlorophyll-a which absorbs blue and
green light and strongly reflects red-edge wavelengths.
A high SFABI score means the ocean surface is reflecting more
red-edge light than blue-green indicating an active bloom.
SFABI above 0.15 is classified as High severity bloom.
SFABI above 0.05 is classified as Medium severity bloom.
SFABI above 0.02 is classified as Low severity bloom.
SFABI below 0.02 is no bloom detected and filtered out.
The current maximum SFABI of 0.4358 indicates areas of High
severity bloom detected from space as of May 8 2026.
The current mean SFABI of 0.1440 indicates moderate bloom
activity across SA coastal waters overall.

NDCI Normalized Difference Chlorophyll Index explained:
NDCI uses bands B4 red and B5 red-edge.
Formula: NDCI equals B5 minus B4 divided by B5 plus B4.
Higher NDCI values indicate higher chlorophyll concentration
which correlates directly with algal bloom intensity.

How satellite compares to ground truth CSV data:
The platform combines two data sources for maximum accuracy.
Sentinel-2 satellite covers the entire SA coastline simultaneously
unlike point-based water sampling which only covers tested sites.
The SA Government Karenia CSV data contains 509 ground truth
readings from water sampling at 512 unique sites since Sep 2025.
The CSV provides exact Karenia cell counts in cells per litre
which the satellite cannot measure directly.
Satellite detects bloom signature across wide areas and gives
early warning before ground sampling confirms it.
Ground truth CSV confirms satellite detections with actual
Karenia cell count measurements at specific beach locations.
The combination of satellite SFABI plus ground truth cell counts
plus live BOM weather data gives the most complete bloom picture
available anywhere in South Australia.

Satellite advantages:
Satellite covers the entire SA coastline in one image pass.
Satellite detects bloom conditions before they become visible
or measurable by ground sampling giving 3 to 5 days earlier warning.
Satellite provides spatial coverage showing where blooms are
spreading across the ocean not just individual beach points.

Satellite limitations:
Cloud cover blocks satellite readings requiring use of the
most recent cloud-free imagery which may be several days old.
Satellite detects chlorophyll signature not exact Karenia cell counts
so ground truth water sampling is needed to confirm the species
and measure exact cell concentrations.
