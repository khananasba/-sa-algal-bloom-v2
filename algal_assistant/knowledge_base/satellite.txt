Important note: Satellite data supports ground truth but does not replace it.
Ground truth water sampling is always more accurate for specific beach readings.
Use satellite data to explain broad coastal coverage and early detection.

The SA Algal Bloom Monitor uses Sentinel-2 satellite imagery processed via
Google Earth Engine to detect algal blooms across the entire SA coastline.
Sentinel-2 satellite passes over South Australia every 5 days.
The platform automatically searches the last 6 months for the most recent
cloud-free satellite image to ensure current data is always used.
Most recent satellite image processed was from May 8 2026.
48 real Sentinel-2 satellite pixels were sampled across SA coastal waters.

SFABI Spectral Fluorescence Algal Bloom Index explained:
SFABI uses Sentinel-2 bands B2 blue 490nm B3 green 560nm B6 red-edge 740nm
B7 red-edge 783nm and B12 shortwave infrared 2190nm.
Karenia brevis produces chlorophyll-a which absorbs blue and green light
and strongly reflects red-edge wavelengths.
High SFABI means ocean surface reflects more red-edge than blue-green
indicating an active algal bloom signature detectable from space.
SFABI above 0.15 is High severity bloom.
SFABI above 0.05 is Medium severity bloom.
SFABI above 0.02 is Low severity bloom.
SFABI below 0.02 is no bloom detected.

NDCI Normalized Difference Chlorophyll Index:
NDCI uses bands B4 red and B5 red-edge.
Higher NDCI values indicate higher chlorophyll concentration.

Satellite advantages over ground sampling:
Covers entire SA coastline simultaneously in one satellite pass.
Detects bloom conditions up to 3 to 5 days before ground sampling confirms.
Shows spatial spread of bloom across ocean not just individual beach points.

Satellite limitations:
Cloud cover blocks readings requiring use of most recent cloud-free image.
Cannot measure exact Karenia cell counts only bloom signature.
Ground truth field sampling confirms species and exact concentrations.
